Web Browser Add-On's

New and upcoming technology on the internet includes RealAudio, Java, and VRML. These new add-ons to your high profile Web Browser give you the ability to do special things, and help to guide the technology into the future. RealAudio gives you the ab ility to listen to music from the Internet in real- time (well, almost real-time). Java is a new animation system similar to HTML that adds on to your Browser and gives you the ability to view animation and scrolling while on a website. VRML is a new vi rtual reality add-on that lets you navigate a web site using "rooms" where you move your mouse or cursor "into" the site, and the page renders on the fly to give you a virtual reality perspective.

Real Audio can be found at http://www.realaudio.com. If you do not have the Browser add-on for Netscape or Mosaic (hopefully you are not using Mosaic...) then go to that URL and choose to download it and make sur e you follow the instructions as you install it. Once you have it installed, there are several sites that now support the RealAudio system... If you have your own site and would like to put real audio on the site so that people can listen to sound while v iewing your own web page, there is an encoder/converter on the RealAudio link that will convert your .WAV and .VOC files into .RA or .AU files (Files used by Real Audio). The links on the RealAudio site include ABC on-line, SUN, and MUSIC EXPRESS just to name a few. There is more and more music added daily, all of it commercial.

Real Audio works on the principle that you do not need to download the complete audio file to play it. The Real Audio system buffers upto 15 seconds of the music file so that it can still get a constant streaming of information (i.e. music) while it c ontinues to play. This gives the Real Audio system the ability to keep up with the constant flow, and there is no dropping of notes occurring. This method of transferring the music over the internet is very effective even for PPP connections down to 1200 BPS.

Java is a new and improved "HTML" scripting mark-up language that gives you the ability to see animation on a website. Currently there are no browsers available for the Windows 95 system. There are browsers available for 32 bit Windows NT, and Solaris, as well as Indigo, and UNIX V (SunOS). If you run Solaris, Solaris X86, Indigo/NeXT, or Windows NT, then make sure you go to the http://java.sun.com site to get the Browser for your particular operating system .

This new technology is definitely something to check out since most web sites will support JAVA shortly. There will be an add-on for Netscape for JAVA made shortly, both for the 16 bit and 32 bit versions. The reasoning there is a new Browser all together is the fact that it is a new markup language, not centered around HTML 3.0, which is what Netscape 1.1 is. VRML is the kewlest add-on for your Browser right now, if you can get hold of it. There are 3 versions, including Windows NT Netscape , Win95 Netscape, and the Solaris series systems. VRML is becoming such an attraction that they are now marketing the add-on for your Browser, trying to make money off this new technology is attractive to the corporate generals who control the scene right now. Since VRML is commercial, there is obviously a pirated version of the Browser add-on on the net. Look for it if you haven't installed it already. Since you can navigate the scenery "virtually", you can also hide doors and such to various F TP sites. This is a major concern for companies doing their best to control piracy, but who cares. They are there to supply us with the software we trade.

There are new VRML sites popping up every day, although the biggest one: http://www.vrml.com has gone down for some reason. It should be back up soon so that you can find the other VRML sites. VRML rendering comes ov er the net, and is based on openGL for Windows NT and Sun systems, and WinG for Win95. Since this is the case, rendering of the graphics is very quick compared to conventional C++ engines (the winG and openGL are integrated within the operating syste m) and HTML engines that would be used to make the real time imaging required for VRML. But, as with all rendering of full page graphics, this system is very processor intensive and can cause your computer to slow down tremendously. The openGL system is much more effective and is about 500% faster than the winG system used by Win95, but Microsoft will not implement openGL into Win95 at all, even in the Win95 updates. Rendering done on a Sparc system running Solaris is almost instantaneous, consid ering it is using Sun's openGL system and the Sparc is also a RISC chip. These new technologies are great add-ons for your internet Web Browser. Check them out. If you need more information on these new technologies, then check out the URL's listed a bove. If you are looking for the VRML add-on, contact _Warlock_ on IRC or look for it around so that you can install it and add it to your Netscape.

When I first thought of interviewing Orion, founder and president of Pirates With Attitudes, I thought, great, I get to learn some stuff from one of the scenes most respected individuals. As the interview with Orion went underway, I got the impression - and so do others who know Orion very well - that he is a careful, sensitive, and a very intelligent man who knows what he is talking about when you speak to him about the scene. The interview went a little something like this :

WWN : Tell the reader a little background info of how Pirates With Attitudes got started (by/whom/why/etc).

ThePrez : "PWA started 3 years ago, With BarManager and myself. We were running INC at the time... We both and the majority of the group agreed that the games scene was heading into a tailspin of kids ruining it with thier ego's. Wanting to be number o ne can do alot to a person. Makes them forget the reason they started this.. And that is #1 to have FUN #2 to get the releases to use..We took a vote of the group, and all the dist sites sysop and all agreed 100% to go with the new look and new name... Th ere were only 2 that didn't go because we felt they were to wrapped into the ego trips.. That was American Gladiator and GateKeeper ... Since then PWA has found itself the resting place of alot of old Pirates like Rambone, Hannibal Lecktor, Rifleman.. In the three years we have absorbed many little groups, and a few large ones also. Like Scotch for instance."

WWN : Can you tell us why PWA isnt such strong competetion amongst the BBS scene? And It's lack of power in the scene overall nowadays?

ThePrez : "Well, PWA like every other group got caught in the Inet Whirlwind. It was this "new" thing and got caught up in it.. As many other groups did. We are heading back to the Basics now as we speak. If you look at the nfo of the new releases we a re throwing out, we are incorporating new BBS boards back into it... But like I stated earlier we just got caught into Inet world...",he continued "Lack of Power? Well really we didn't lose any power. Power isn't resolved because of the amount of relea se that one group does. Its the over all of the scene, we still have all our contacts and still have the major people in the scene connected with us... So we really didn't lose Power as you use that term.."

WWN : What do you plan/expect of PWA in the upcoming months/years? ThePrez : "Well after Blackhawk got busted, I decided for PWA to take a `reality' break, that lasted for 6 months. We are getting back into full swing now.. Blackie is coming back... W e are just going to have fun and fuck everyone else.. And when we do it we are just going to it with a TUDE..."

WWN : Can you give us just a little recap of what happend with the busting of BlackHawk/TAG? ThePrez : "Sure, He got busted for MS and Novell shit on his board... Kinda funny how it happened but that is another article you can ask him about... But in the short of it, he got all his equipment seized and phone records taken.. MS is looking for the neighborhood of 150K of reconcilation for `thier' troubles... He is and will be fine thru all this...PWA will survive and continue..."

WWN : Ok, Do you have any comments on regard to MikeySoft's Article about the "State of The Scene" in last week's WWN?

ThePrez : "You can print my comment, and I hope you do...Mikeysoft and I have been working for months on the concept of bring the scene to a all time high. And that is to form UNITY a group just one group in the scene...I believe in what MikeySoft had to say 100%.
