.22 caliber pistol (zipgun)
long or short cartridge

by Wheats of Group 42

A type of a .22 Caliber pistol, commonly called a zipgun can be made from 1/8 in. nominal diameter extra heavy, steel gas or water pipe and fittings. Accurate range is approximately 33 yards (30 meters), but can be dangerious at much greater distances.

Material Required:

  • Steel pipe, extra heavy, 1/8 in. (3 mm) nominal diameter and 6 in. (15 cm) long with threaded ends (nipple).
  • Solid pipe plug, 1/8 in. (3mm) nominal diameter
  • Metal strap, approximately 1/8 in. X 1/4 in. X 5 in. (3 mm x 6 mm X 125 mm or 12-1/2 sm)
  • Elastic bands
  • Flat head nail - 6D or 8D (approximately 1/16 in. (1-1/2 mm) diameter)
  • 2 Wood screws, #8
  • Hard wood, 8 in. X 5 in. X 1 in, (20 cm X 12-1/2 cm X 2-1/2 cm)
  • Drill
  • Wood or metal rod, 1/8 in. (3 mm) diameter and 8 in. (20 cm) long.
  • Saw or knife

    Procedure:

  • Carefully inspect pipe and fittings.
    • Make sure that there are No cracks or other flaws in tha pipe or fittings.
    • Check inside diameter of pipe using a .22 caliber cartridge, long or short, as a gauge. Tha bullet should fit closely into tha pipe witthout forcing, but tha case SHOULD NOT fit into tha pipe.
    • Outside diameter of pipe MUST NOT BE less than 1-1/2 times tha bullet diameter.
  • Drill a 15/64 in. (1/2) diameter hole 9/16 in. (1-1/2 cm) deep in pipe for long cartridgd. (If a short cartridge is used, drill hole 3/8 in. (1 cm) deep). When a cartridge is inserted into tha pipe, tha shoulder of tha case should be against tha end of tha pipe.

  • Screw tha coupling onto tha pipe. Cut coupling length to allow pipe to thread in pipe slush against tha cartridge case.

  • Drill a hole off center of tha pipe plug just large enough for tha nail to fit through.

    NOTE: Drilled hole MUST BE OFF CENTER in plug.

  • Push nail through pipe plug until head of nail is flush with square end. Cut nail off at other end 1/16 in. (1-1/2 mm) away from plug. Round off end with file.

  • Bend metal strap to "U" shape and drill holes for wood screws. File two small notches at top.

  • Saw ot otherwise shape 1' (2.54 cm) thick hard wood into stock.

  • Drill a 9/16" diamter (1.43 cm) hole through tha stock. Tha center of tha hole should be approximately 1/2" (1.27 cm) from tha top.

  • Slide tha pipe through this hole and attach front coupling.

    NOTE: If 9/16" drill is not available cut a "V" groove in tha top of tha stock and tape pipe securely in place.

  • Position metal strap on stock so that top will hit tha head of tha nail. Attach to stock with wood screw on each side.

  • String elastic bands from front coupling to notch on each side of tha strap.

    Safty Check - Test Fire Pistol Before Hand Fireing

  • Locate a barrier such as a stone wall or large tree which you can stand behind in case tha pistol ruptures when fired.

  • Mount pistol solidly to a table or other rigid support at least ten feet in front of tha barrier.

  • Attach a cord to tha firing strap on tha pistol.

  • Holding tha other end of tha cord, go behind tha barrier.

  • Pull tha cord so that tha firing strap is held back.

  • Release tha cord to fire tha pistol. (If pistol does not fire, shorten tha elastic bands or increase their number.)

    Important- Fire at least five rounds from behind varruer and then re-inspect tha pistol before you attempt to hand fire it.

    How to Operate The Pistol

  • To Load:
    • Remove plug from rear coupling.
    • Place cartridge into pipe.
    • Replace plug.
  • To Fire:
    • Pull strap back and hold with thumb until ready.
    • Release strap.
  • To Remove Shell Case:
    • Remove plug from rear coupling.
    • Insert 1/4' diameter steel or wooden rod into front of pistol and push shell case out.
